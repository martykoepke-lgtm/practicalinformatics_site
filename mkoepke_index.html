The "Vibe Coding" Trap

You've seen the hype. "Build an app in 60 minutes!" So you tried Bolt, Replit, or Lovable. Maybe you got something that looked like an app.

Then reality hit:

  • ✗ This is taking way more prompts than you expected — and the AI credits are adding up
  • ✗ Something broke and you had no idea why
  • ✗ You couldn't deploy it to a real domain
  • ✗ Payments? Privacy policies? App store submission? Nobody mentioned those.
  • ✗ You had a demo, not a product

So you gave up. Or started over. Again.

The tools are powerful. But without context, you're burning time and money guessing.

Context-First App Building

Questions

Do I need coding experience?

No. But you should be comfortable learning new tools and willing to read documentation when needed. I'll teach you how to understand what the AI generates.

What kind of app can I build?

Internal tools, MVPs, simple SaaS products, portfolio projects, client-facing apps. Not games or complex enterprise platforms.

What tools will we use?

Cursor (AI-assisted coding), Supabase (database + auth), Vercel (hosting), and Claude or ChatGPT for prompting. All have generous free tiers.

What's the time commitment?

3-5 hours per week: one 60-minute call plus 2-4 hours building between sessions.

What if I don't finish in 8 weeks?

We keep going until you ship. No extra charge.

What if my idea isn't right?

That's part of the process. We'll figure that out in the first two weeks — before you've invested build time in the wrong direction.

Why only three clients at a time?

Because this is real 1:1 work, not a course you watch alone. I'm in the details with you. That only works with a small number of people.

Activiteez

Activity and resource management for multi-location organizations

The Problem

Senior living facilities manage dozens of recurring activities across multiple buildings — often with spreadsheets, paper calendars, and guesswork about supplies.

The Solution

A platform that handles scheduling, inventory tracking with automatic consumption, attendance management, and multi-building data isolation. Built for the activity director who doesn't have an IT department.

What I Learned

Multi-tenant architecture sounds abstract until you're debugging why Building A can see Building B's bingo cards. I learned to think in contexts — and to let AI help me catch the edge cases I'd miss.

GovernIQ

Enterprise work management and capacity planning

The Problem

Organizations tracking dozens of initiatives across multiple teams struggle to see who's overloaded, what's stuck, and where to focus. Spreadsheets break down. Visibility disappears.

The Solution

A configurable platform for initiative tracking, governance workflows, and real-time capacity analytics. Fully customizable fields, weights, and branding — no code changes required.

What I Learned

The hardest part of enterprise software isn't the features — it's making it flexible enough to fit how different organizations actually work. I built a system where admins can change everything without touching code.

VytalPath Academy

Training platform for new healthcare staff

The Problem

New front office staff face an overwhelming first day — medical jargon, insurance workflows, HIPAA compliance, clinic procedures. The result is anxiety, mistakes, and high turnover.

The Solution

A training and reference platform with 1,000+ healthcare terms in plain language, step-by-step workflows, HIPAA modules, and smart search. Accessible on phone or desktop.

What I Learned

Training isn't about dumping information — it's about being there at the moment someone needs an answer. I built for the person Googling "what does PRN mean" during their first week.
